# Phase 2: Contrarian Debate - Web3 Luxury Watch Auth

## 🥊 Round 1: Cross-Examination (Bắn Tỉa Lẫn Nhau)

**CTO attacks CMO:**
"CMO wants to partner with gray market dealers. This is a massive 'Counterfeit Entry Point.' If a dealer colludes to put our 'Verified' NFC tag on a fake watch, our entire platform's trust is destroyed. How do we verify the *verifier*?"

**COO attacks CTO:**
"CTO proposes 'AI Image Recognition movement fingerprinting.' This requires high-res, specialized cameras. No 'Watch Enthusiast' at home has a $1k macro-lens to verify their own watch. We are building a product that only professionals can use. Can we make this work on a standard iPhone?"

**CMO attacks COO:**
"COO suggests 'Secondary Market Royalties' (1%). Web3 users *hate* mandatory royalties—it's the reason OpenSea is dying. If we force a 1% fee, people will just trade the physical watch and the 'Digital Passport' separately off-chain. Why not a 'Valuation Update' fee instead?"

**CPO Direction:**
"CTO, the focus must be on **'Blind Verification'**—multiple dealers must verify a piece before it gets an 'Elite' status. COO, the iPhone is the only device that matters; find a way to use the existing macro-mode. CMO, the 'Valuation Update' is much better; keep the secondary trade free to drive adoption."

## 🛡️ Round 2: Defense & Pivot (Bảo Vệ & Cải Tiến)

**CTO Defense:**
"I will pivot to **'Crowdsourced Verifier Protocol'**. For high-value pieces (>$10k), we require 2 independent certified watchmakers to scan the NFC tag before the 'Authenticity' state is 'Confirmed.' I'll optimize the AI fingerprinting to work with iPhone 13+ Pro macro cameras."

**CMO Defense:**
"Pivoting from 'Secondary Royalties' to **'The Heirloom Upgrade'**. The basic passport is free, but users pay a $25 annual fee for 'Insurance-Ready Valuation Reports' and 'Historical Registry' access. This creates recurring revenue without taxing the liquidity of the asset."

**COO Defense:**
"Accepting the **'Watchmaker Oracle' model**. We'll provide the NFC tags to certified watchmakers for a deposit. This creates a 'Quality-of-Supply' gate. Revenue will come from the **'Auth-as-a-Service'** fee split between the platform and the local watchmaker ($10 each)."
