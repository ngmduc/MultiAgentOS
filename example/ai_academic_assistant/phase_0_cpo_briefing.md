# Phase 0: CPO Briefing - AI Academic Writing Assistant

## 1. Product Briefing & CPO Assessment
**Project:** AI Writing Assistant for Academic Researchers (Grants/Papers).
**Core Mission:** Eradicate "AI Hallucinations" from scholarly work to protect researcher reputation.

### The "Creative-First" Assessment:
Researchers are terrified of AI because of fake citations. We aren't building a wrapper for ChatGPT; we're building a **Verification Overlay** for Scite/Elicit/Semantic Scholar. The product is a "Citation-First" editor where text cannot be written unless it's tethered to a verified PDF snippet.

### Key Hooks (LTV/CAC Efficiency):
- **Evidence-Gated Drafting:** The AI can only generate sentences based on selected search results in a side-panel.
- **RFP-to-Draft Mapper:** Instantly aligns research objectives with Grant Agency (NSF/NIH) requirements.
- **Hallucination Heatmap:** Highlights any sentence that lacks strong external evidence.

## 2. Raw Data Audit Trail (Research)
- **Reddit/Hacker News Insights:** "I love AI for outlines but I spend 4 hours checking its fake sources."
- **Competitors:** Jenni AI (Good UI, still hallucinates), Scite.ai (Great data, poor writing interface).
- **Market Gap:** The bridge between "High-Quality Scholarly Search" and "Generative Drafting."

## 3. High-Level Requirements
- **Swarm Activation:**
    - **CTO:** Architect a RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) system specifically for PubMed/Semantic Scholar APIs.
    - **CMO:** Focus on "Ph.D. Productivity"—position it as a co-author, not a "cheat" tool.
    - **COO:** Subscription model targeting individual lab budgets ($30-$50/mo).
